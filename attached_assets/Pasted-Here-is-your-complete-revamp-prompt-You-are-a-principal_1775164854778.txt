## THE PROBLEM WITH THE CURRENT STRUCTURE

The current app has 12 linear "phases" that feel like arbitrary gates. Users have no idea what they are learning or why. There are no lessons — just challenges that assume knowledge the user was never given. Concepts like lots, pips, stop loss, R:R ratio, and position sizing are used in challenges without ever being explained. The phase system is broken — users skip phases after 2 trades. XP inflates too fast. Challenge criteria are either stubs or not validated at all. ARIA repeats the same generic messages. Nothing teaches anything.

Throw away the phase structure entirely. Replace it with the Academy structure described below.

---

## THE NEW STRUCTURE: TRADING ACADEMY

Three tiers. Each tier has modules. Each module has lessons followed by one mission. Lessons teach. Missions prove. You cannot access a mission without completing its lessons. You cannot access the next module without completing the current mission. This is the law — no exceptions except Dev Mode.

Every lesson is a 20 to 40 second interactive experience that plays directly on the chart. ARIA narrates with typewriter animation while animated overlays draw on the canvas in real time showing exactly what the lesson is about. The user watches, reads, and interacts. Then they take the mission — a real trade on the live chart that proves they understood the lesson.

## TIER 1 — RECRUIT

*ARIA introduction line: "You know nothing about markets yet. That is fine. I will build your foundation from zero."*

### Module 1: The Market
- **Lesson 1 — What is price:** ARIA narrates while the chart zooms in on 5 candles. An animated arrow traces price moving up and down. Caption: "Price is simply the last amount someone agreed to pay. Every tick is a transaction."
- **Lesson 2 — What is a candle:** ARIA draws animated labels on one candle — open, close, high, low. The body fills in, then wicks extend. Caption: "Each candle is one time period. The body is where price started and ended. The wicks show how far it reached."
- **Lesson 3 — Bull vs Bear candle:** Two candles animate side by side — one green filling upward, one red filling downward. Caption: "Green means buyers won that period. Red means sellers won. Simple as that."
- **Lesson 4 — What is a pip:** ARIA draws a bracket on the chart between two price levels with an animated measurement line. The number 1.00 appears next to it. Caption: "A pip is the smallest standard price movement. On Gold, moving from 1950.00 to 1951.00 is 100 pips."
- **Mission 1:** Open any BUY trade and close it. ARIA guides every single click with animated arrows pointing at the exact UI element to use. The BUY button pulses. The Close button pulses when trade is open. Pass on any outcome.

### Module 2: Reading Candles
- **Lesson 1 — The Doji:** ARIA highlights a doji candle on the live chart with a glowing rectangle. Animated labels show equal wicks. Caption: "A Doji has almost no body. Buyers and sellers ended exactly where they started. This means indecision — neither side won."
- **Lesson 2 — The Hammer:** ARIA highlights a hammer candle. Animated arrow points to the long lower wick. Caption: "A Hammer has a long lower wick. Sellers pushed price down hard, but buyers fought back and closed near the top. This is rejection of lower prices."
- **Lesson 3 — The Engulfing:** ARIA highlights two consecutive candles where the second completely engulfs the first. An animated bracket shows the second candle is larger. Caption: "An Engulfing candle completely swallows the previous candle. This is a momentum shift — the new side overwhelmed the old."
- **Lesson 4 — What wicks mean:** ARIA draws on three candles showing different wick lengths with animated length comparisons. Caption: "Long wicks mean the market tried to go somewhere and got rejected. Always ask: where is the wick pointing, and where is it coming from?"
- **Mission 2:** ARIA marks a specific candle pattern on the live chart with a glowing highlight. User must identify whether it is bullish or bearish by clicking a BUY or SELL button before placing the trade. Then hold the trade for at least 3 candles.

### Module 3: Trend
- **Lesson 1 — What is a trend:** ARIA draws animated arrows connecting swing highs and swing lows on the chart. Green arrows for higher highs, higher lows. Caption: "An uptrend is a series of higher highs and higher lows. Price is making progress upward. Downtrend is the opposite."
- **Lesson 2 — Market structure:** ARIA marks HH, HL, LH, LL labels on the last 20 candles of the live chart with animated text appearing one by one. Caption: "Structure is the skeleton of the market. Before every trade, identify the structure. It tells you who is in control."
- **Lesson 3 — Trend reversal:** ARIA animates a scenario where a higher low is broken — draws a circle around the break, arrow showing the shift. Caption: "A trend reversal begins when structure breaks. A higher low that fails to form, or a swing low that is broken — these are your warning signs."
- **Lesson 4 — Why fighting the trend is fatal:** ARIA shows an animated price sequence — a strong uptrend, user sells into it, price continues up, account damaged. Caption: "The trend is the most powerful force in the market. Every trade against it requires the trend to be wrong. Most of the time, the trend is not wrong."
- **Mission 3:** Wait for the market regime badge to show UPTREND. Open a BUY trade. Hold it for a minimum of 5 candles. Close it in profit. ARIA tells the user exactly what to watch for and when to enter.

### Module 4: Support and Resistance
- **Lesson 1 — What is a level:** ARIA animates price touching the same horizontal area 3 times — each touch highlighted with a pulse. A horizontal line draws itself. Caption: "A support or resistance level is a price area where the market has reacted before. The more touches, the stronger the level."
- **Lesson 2 — Support vs Resistance:** ARIA animates a price sequence — price bounces off support, then breaks through, then retests from above as new support. Caption: "Resistance is a ceiling. Support is a floor. When price breaks through one, it often flips — the old ceiling becomes the new floor."
- **Lesson 3 — Zones not lines:** ARIA erases a thin line on the chart and replaces it with a shaded band. Caption: "Levels are not laser lines. They are zones. Price rarely respects an exact number — it respects an area. Trade the zone, not the line."
- **Lesson 4 — Trading from a level:** ARIA annotates an entry setup on the chart — price approaching support, a rejection candle forming, entry arrow appearing. Caption: "The highest probability entries happen when price returns to a level it has respected before. Wait for the rejection. Then enter."
- **Mission 4:** Open a trade within 5 pips of an active S/R zone shown on the chart. Stop loss must be set on the other side of the zone. ARIA highlights the valid zones with animated pulses to show where they are.

## TIER 2 — TRADER

*ARIA introduction line: "You can read the market. Now I will teach you how to trade it without destroying your account."*

### Module 5: Your First Real Setup
- **Lesson 1 — What is a Stop Loss:** ARIA animates a trade going against the user — price falls, the SL line catches it, trade closes, small loss. Then plays the same trade without SL — price keeps falling, account devastated. Caption: "A stop loss is your exit when you are wrong. Every professional uses one on every single trade. No exceptions."
- **Lesson 2 — What is a Take Profit:** ARIA animates price reaching a TP level and the trade closing automatically with a positive result. Caption: "A take profit locks in your gain automatically. You do not need to watch the screen. Set it and let the plan execute."
- **Lesson 3 — R:R Ratio explained:** ARIA renders an animated R:R bar on screen — red segment for risk, green segment for reward. Shows 1:1, then 2:1, then 3:1. Caption: "R:R is how much you make vs how much you risk. At 2:1, you make $200 when right and lose $100 when wrong. You only need to be right 34% of the time to profit."
- **Lesson 4 — The math of 2:1 over 100 trades:** ARIA shows an animated table — 50 wins at 2:1 vs 50 losses at 1:1. Running total column fills in. Caption: "At 2:1 R:R with a 50% win rate, you are profitable over time. This is the edge. Protect it on every trade."
- **Mission 5:** Enter a trade with both SL and TP set. TP must be at minimum 2 times the SL distance. Both lines must be visible on the chart before submission is allowed.

### Module 6: Position Sizing
- **Lesson 1 — What is a lot:** ARIA renders an animated diagram showing lot sizes — micro 0.01, mini 0.1, standard 1.0 — with pip value calculations appearing for each. Caption: "A lot is the size of your trade. A standard lot on Gold means each pip is worth $1. A 0.01 micro lot means each pip is worth $0.01."
- **Lesson 2 — The 1% rule:** ARIA animates the math live on screen. $10,000 balance. 1% circle highlights $100. Caption: "Never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade. On a $10,000 account that is $100. This is not optional. This is survival."
- **Lesson 3 — Calculating lot size:** ARIA walks through the formula with animated numbers. Stop loss = 20 pips. Risk = $100. Lot size = Risk ÷ (SL pips × pip value) = 0.10 lots. Caption: "Your lot size is determined by your stop loss distance, not by how confident you feel."
- **Lesson 4 — Drawdown compounding:** ARIA shows an animated chart of account equity — undisciplined trader losing 5% per trade vs disciplined trader losing 1% per trade. After 10 losses the difference is catastrophic. Caption: "Losing 10 trades in a row happens to every trader. The question is whether your account survives it."
- **Mission 6:** Complete 3 consecutive trades. Every trade must have a stop loss. Every trade risk must be under 1.5% of current balance. If any trade breaks the rule, the counter resets to zero and ARIA explains exactly what was wrong.

### Module 7: Entry Timing
- **Lesson 1 — Anticipation vs confirmation:** ARIA animates two traders — one entering before a candle closes (anticipating), price reverses against them. One waiting for the candle to close above the level (confirming), price continues in their direction. Caption: "Anticipation feels smart. It is usually wrong. Confirmation is slower. It is usually right."
- **Lesson 2 — The confirmation candle:** ARIA draws on the chart — a resistance level, a candle that closes above it with a full body, an entry arrow appearing after the close. Caption: "Wait for the candle to CLOSE, not just touch. A close is a commitment. A touch is a test."
- **Lesson 3 — Pullback entries:** ARIA animates a breakout, then a pullback to the broken level, then a continuation. Entry arrow appears at the pullback. Caption: "The best time to enter after a breakout is not during the spike. It is during the retest. Lower risk, higher reward, cleaner entry."
- **Lesson 4 — Patience as a skill:** ARIA displays a counter: "Trades skipped this session: 4. Trades taken: 1. Win rate: 100%." Caption: "The best traders are defined as much by the trades they do not take as by the ones they do. Patience is not passive. It is a decision."
- **Mission 7:** Wait for a candle to close above a resistance zone or below a support zone shown on the chart. Enter in the breakout direction within 2 candles of the close. SL below the zone. TP at 2:1.

### Module 8: Moving Averages
- **Lesson 1 — What is an EMA:** ARIA animates the EMA 21 line appearing over the candles, smoothly following price. Caption: "An Exponential Moving Average tracks the average price weighted toward recent candles. It tells you the current direction of momentum."
- **Lesson 2 — EMA as dynamic support:** ARIA highlights 3 instances on the chart where price pulled back to EMA 21 and bounced. Circles on each bounce. Caption: "In a strong trend, the EMA acts as a moving support or resistance line. Price pulls back to it, respects it, and continues."
- **Lesson 3 — EMA 9 and EMA 21 crossover:** ARIA animates the two EMAs crossing — golden cross where 9 crosses above 21 highlighted in teal, death cross where 9 crosses below 21 highlighted in red. Caption: "When the faster EMA crosses the slower one, momentum has shifted. This is your trend filter."
- **Lesson 4 — Using EMA as a filter not a signal:** Caption: "Never trade just because EMAs crossed. Use the crossover to confirm the trend direction, then wait for a price action entry from a level."
- **Mission 8:** Open a BUY trade only when EMA 9 is above EMA 21 on the live chart. Entry must be on a pullback to EMA 21 or EMA 9. Stop loss below the EMA. ARIA highlights when the setup is valid with a pulsing annotation.

### Module 9: RSI
- **Lesson 1 — What is RSI:** ARIA animates the RSI panel appearing below the chart. The line moves between 0 and 100. Caption: "RSI measures momentum — how fast and how far price has moved. Above 70 means overbought. Below 30 means oversold."
- **Lesson 2 — Overbought and oversold:** ARIA highlights moments on the chart where RSI hit 70 and price reversed, and RSI hit 30 and price reversed. Caption: "Extreme RSI readings do not mean sell or buy immediately. They mean the move may be exhausted. Wait for price confirmation."
- **Lesson 3 — RSI divergence:** ARIA draws on the chart — price making a higher high while RSI makes a lower high. Animated lines connecting the peaks. Caption: "Divergence is when price and RSI disagree. Price goes higher but RSI goes lower — momentum is fading even though price looks strong. This often precedes a reversal."
- **Lesson 4 — RSI as a filter:** Caption: "Use RSI to confirm or reject entries. If you want to buy and RSI is at 78, wait. If you want to buy and RSI is at 42 and rising, that is confluence."
- **Mission 9:** Open 2 trades with stop losses that both close in profit. At entry, RSI must not be in the opposite extreme — no buying above RSI 65, no selling below RSI 35.

## HOW LESSONS WORK — TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION

Each lesson is implemented as an `ARIALesson` object. When a lesson starts:

1. The chart pauses price generation or continues but overlays a semi-transparent dim layer
2. ARIA's panel expands and she delivers her narration via typewriter animation
3. Simultaneously, canvas annotations animate on the chart — lines draw themselves, labels appear, candles highlight, brackets animate
4. When narration completes a "Next" button appears or it auto-advances after 5 seconds
5. User can replay any completed lesson from the module overview screen

Each lesson object contains:
- `id`, `title`, `narration` — ARIA's full spoken text
- `annotationSequence` — array of timed canvas drawing commands that execute in sync with narration
- `duration` — expected lesson length in seconds
- `replayable` — boolean

All 13 modules × average 4 lessons = approximately 52 lessons total. Every single one must be implemented with real canvas animations. No placeholders.

---

## HOW MISSIONS WORK — TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION

Each mission is a `TradingMission` object with:
- `id`, `title`, `briefing` — ARIA's mission briefing
- `criteria` — array of `MissionCriterion` objects, each with an `id`, `description`, `check` function, and `isMet` boolean
- `progressDisplay` — how to show live progress (counter, checklist, percentage)
- `xpReward` — scales by module: Module 1 = 100 XP, Module 13 = 1000 XP
- `successMessage` — ARIA's specific success speech referencing what was done correctly
- `failureMessage` — ARIA's specific failure speech with exact explanation of what was missing
- `ariaGuidanceMessages` — array of 6+ live messages ARIA sends during mission execution, specific to the mission's concept

Mission validation runs on every trade close and on every trade open attempt. ARIA must never let a user submit a trade that violates mission requirements without first warning them in amber mode. If they override the warning (some missions allow trades that are not perfect), ARIA flags the violation but lets it proceed and notes it in the mission debrief.
