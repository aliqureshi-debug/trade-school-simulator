## STEP 5 — PRICE ENGINE (COMPLETE REBUILD)

Rebuild `chartEngine.ts` to produce XAU/USD-like price action that feels real:

**Market regimes:**
- UPTREND: Positive drift 0.3–0.8 pips per candle average. Pullbacks of 3–7 candles that do NOT break the last swing low. After each pullback, new high is made. Structure is visually clear: genuine higher highs and higher lows.
- DOWNTREND: Mirror of uptrend. Genuine lower highs and lower lows.
- RANGE: Price oscillates between two defined boundaries 15–30 pips apart. Mean reversion bias. Wicks extend toward boundaries and reject. Occasionally false breaks by 2–3 pips before returning.
- BREAKOUT: Triggered from a range when price approaches boundary with 3 consecutive candles in the breakout direction. Sharp 20–35 pip move over 4–6 candles, then a retest of the broken level (price returns to within 3 pips of breakout level) before continuing.
- VOLATILE: Short regime 8–15 candles. High ATR, large wicks, no clear direction. Appears during news events.

**Regime transitions:**
- Never abrupt. A 10–15 candle transition period between regimes where characteristics of both appear.
- The current regime and candles remaining in it are tracked in engine state and exposed to the UI.

**Volatility clustering:**
- ATR (Average True Range) varies organically. Quiet periods (small candles, tight wicks) transition gradually into expansion (large candles, extended wicks). Never mechanical uniform randomness.

**News spike events:**
- Every 50–80 candles, a news event triggers.
- 3 candles before the spike, ATR increases slightly and ARIA warns "Volatility event detected — consider waiting for the move to complete before entering."
- The spike itself: 20–40 pip move in a random direction over 2–3 candles.
- Followed by 50% partial retracement over the next 4–6 candles.
- A "NEWS" label appears on the chart at the spike candle.

**S/R calculation:**
- Pure pivot logic as described in chart section above.
- Recalculate every 20 candles.
- Expose as a clean function that returns an array of SRZone objects with: price, type (support/resistance), strength (how many times tested), and first detected timestamp.
